INTRODUCTION

Welcome to our website! Here you can find information about our favourite fantasy book "A Game of Thrones". With the navigation bar on top of the page you can navigate to different parts of this website where you will find different kinds of information. The 'Homepage' is where you are right now and where you can find general information regarding this site. Under the 'Story' tab we will provide you with a summary of the book. On the 'Characters' page you will find some more information regarding the most important Characters in the story. Under 'World' we show you where the story takes place and tell you a bit more about each area. To learn more about the author of this book you can go to the 'Author' page. On each page you will find a sidebar with some facts and figures about the praticular subject of the page. We hope you have a pleasant stay!

ABOUT THIS WEBSITE

This website was made as the first practical for the course 'Webtechnology' at the university of Utrecht. In short the assignment was to develop a static website about our favourite book containing at least five pages of meaningful content. Since we could't identify a book that all of us had read we decided to identify a movie or show we all had seen which was based on a book. This resulted in the decision of making this website about 'A Game of Thrones' which is the first book in a popular series written by George R. R. Martin and of which also a tv show was made. Some more of the requirements for this website included the use of images, having links within, between and to external pages and to include a table. You can a lot of image use on the 'Characters' page. On the 'World' page you can find links within the page in the form of an interactive map. If you click on any general area on the map you will be directed to the corresponding information box. Links between images are used for navigation between the pages of this site. Clicking on one of the navigation tags on top of this screen will direct you to the corresponding page on this website. Links to external pages are mostly used on informational pages to direct you to the source of the information.
